After I graduated high school I continued to work with my dad remotely while attending Texas A&M University. My dad got a contract with CBS NFL, so every Thursday through Sunday we would remote in to all the CBS trucks at each site and setup a storage system to capture all the individual clips from each camera feed of each game, and then we would send them directly to New York where they could use the clips later or immediately for highlight reels and other purposes. It came with its challenges but it was an amazing opportunity that I got to embrace! After college I went on to work at Baylor Scott & White in Frisco where I watch heart rhythms on the Post-Surgical Unit, communicate with doctors and nurses to get orders into the system, and also work as a patient care tech. Being the computer savy guy I am I also took on the role of being the on floor IT guy and anytime there were any computer problems they would call me before the IT department. As much as I have enjoyed working in healthcare, I've always wanted to get back to my love of technology so I stated taking programming classes whcih eventually lead me to my interest in data analytics, with plans to one day become a data scientist.
